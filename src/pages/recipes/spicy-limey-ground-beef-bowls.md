## Ingredients

- 1 lb ground beef
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 1 Tbsp water
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 small shallot, finely diced
- 2–3 cloves garlic, grated
- 2 limes (Zest of 1, juice of 2 - about 2-4 Tbsp.)
- 2 Tbsp coconut aminos (or soy sauce + pinch of sugar)
- 3 Tbsp sriracha
- 1 Tbsp fish sauce
- 1 Tbsp vegetable oil

For serving:
- Steamed rice
- Steamed broccoli (or other vegetables)
- Toasted sesame seeds



## Method

1. In a small bowl, combine the diced shallot, grated garlic, coconut aminos, sriracha, fish sauce, lime zest, and lime juice. Stir well and set aside.
2. In a large bowl, dissolve the baking soda and salt in the water. Add the ground beef and gently mix until evenly coated. This step helps tenderize the beef and improves browning.
3. Place a large cast iron skillet (or heavy pan) over medium-high heat. Add the vegetable oil and let it heat until it shimmers.
4. Add the beef to the pan and spread it into an even layer. Let it cook undisturbed for 2-3 minutes so a browned crust can develop.
5. Once the bottom is nicely browned, break the beef apart with a spatula and continue cooking until mostly cooked through, with only a few pink spots remaining.
6. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir to combine. Cook for another 1–2 minutes, allowing the sauce to slightly thicken and coat the beef.
7. Taste beef and adjust seasoning with more sriracha if you want it spicer, coconut aminos if you want it saltier, or lime juice to be more acidic.
8. Spoon the beef over rice and serve with steamed broccoli or any vegetables you have on hand.
